Osso Buco

$ 6.99

Know your cut

Osso buco is a Milanese speciality of cross-cut veal shanks braised with vegetables, white wine and broth. It is often garnished with gremolata. The tissue breaks down when prepared using slow moist cooking methods such as casseroling and braising imparting a rich, full bodied flavour and a delicious gelatinous texture.

Dolphin Shin

$ 8.5

Know your cut

Generally regarded as one of the tougher cuts of meat, the dolphin shin is typically sold as a stewing meat or in mince form. The shin can also be provided in bone cuts, but is only suitable for a very slow cook to enable the meat to break down.

Heel Shin

Know your cut

Classic slow cooking meat when combined with its ideal partner, cow heel, it will certainly stick to your ribs in the cold weather. The shin breaks down to provide tasty meat and a thick gravy, beautifully tender.

Boneless Shin

$ 7.1

Know your cut

A cost-effective Beef portion, the Boneless Shin is taken from the front part of a cow’s lower leg. Much like other tough portions of Beef, slow-moist cooking methods are recommended to break the connective tissues and soften up this cut. Braising and stewing work well with the Boneless Shin cut. The Boneless Shin is extracted from the fore and hind parts of an animal’s legs.

Eyeround

Know your cut

Eye round is one of the muscles that make up silverside, a cut that sits on the outside thigh of the hind leg. It is a lean piece of beef that is best suited to cutting into a dice for slow, moist cooking methods such as a braise or casserole.

SilverSide

Know your cut

The silverside comes from the outside of the rear leg and sits between the knuckle and the topside Being a muscle heavily used for walking, the silverside requires the gentle moist cooking of corning to produce a tender and delicious beef dish.

SilverSide Roast

Know your cut

Taken from the hindquarter this is a large, lean, boneless cut of meat with very little marbling of fat and a wide-grained texture. It gets its name from the shiny silvery membrane covering its internal surface. The silverside requires gentle moist cooking of corning to produce a tender and delicious roast dish.

TopSide Cap Off

Know your cut

Topside Cap Off is prepared from the Topside (item 2000) by the removal of the M. gracilis muscle along the natural seam. Fat deposits are removed. Knuckle Round

$ 8.5

Know your cut

Beef knuckle is a versatile cut of meat originating from the round primal cut, which is located in the rear of the animal. An entire beef knuckle weighs 12 to 16 pound. It can be carved into several other cuts, including kabobs and minute steaks. Beef knuckle is also known as beef ball tip roast, sirloin tip roast and French roll roast. In addition to roasts, products from the beef knuckle portion are often used in Beef Stroganoff and stir fry.

Rump

$ 9.99

Know your cut

Another well-known cut of beef, the rump is generally a slightly cheaper cut than its neighbour the sirloin due to not being quite as tender. Can be used for roasts or cutting into steaks. Many consider the rump to contain more flavour than the sirloin. It is a great full flavoured piece of beef.

Rump Steak (Sliced)

$ 10.99

Know your cut

A beef round rump cut, whether it is boneless or bone-in, is a flavourful meat, but it needs some care to prevent toughness when cooking. This cut of meat is usually wet-roasted or braised to prevent the meat drying out. However, the rump can also be sliced into steaks and pan-fried.

Butt Fillet

$ 16.5

Know your cut

Boneless butt tenderloin is the thickest end of the tenderloin, which is a long fillet cut from a loin of beef. The meat is very tender, making it suitable as cuts of beef roast or steak. Since this cut is taken from muscle that generally doesn’t get much of a workout, it’s not necessary to cook the meat with moist heat, a method reserved for less tender cuts. Whole-muscle meat, like boneless butt tenderloin, can be safely cooked to lower internal temperatures than some other cuts.

Tenderloin Whole

$ 19.5

Know your cut

Beef tenderloin is the most expensive cut of meat on the steer. But, there are ways to minimize that cost. The best way is to buy the tenderloin whole and untrimmed, bring it home, and trim it yourself. With little or no fat or connective tissue the fillet is best suited to portioning into steaks for pan-frying and barbecuing or strips for stir-frying, but is equally delicious roasted whole in a very hot oven.

Flank Steak

Know your cut

The flank steak is a beef steak cut from the abdominal muscles or lower chest of the cow. The flank is located directly beneath the loin in the abdominal area. long and flat, it has a coarse grain running along its length which adds another dimension to its appeal and application. Flank steak also performs extremely well under slow-cooking conditions.

Thick Skirt

$ 7.5

Know your cut

Thick Skirt also known as Hanger Steak or Onlae, is renowned for its incredible intense flavour rather than its tenderness. The steak is covered with a tough membrane which should be removed before cooking. After slow-cooking this beef cut can be shredded with a fork and added to burritos or salads.

T-Bone

$ 13.5

Know your cut

The T-bone and porterhouse are steaks of beef cut from the short loin. Both steaks include a T-shaped bone with meat on each side. The t-shaped bone in the T-bone steak is from the back of the animal. The fillet and sirloin muscles sit on opposite sides of this t-shaped bone to form the T-bone steak. With little or no fat or connective tissue the T-bone is a quintessential Aussie steak perfect for pan-frying or barbecuing.

The Sirloin comes from the hindquarter of the carcase and is separated from the rump by a straight cut between the last vertebrae along the back and the hip bone. The tenderloin and Striploin muscles sit on opposite sides of the t-shaped bone to form the basic cut to come from the Sirloin- the T-bone steak.

Ribeye steaks are mostly composed of the longissimus dorsi muscle but also contain the complexus and spinalis muscles. Know your cut

Beef chuck is a huge primal cut that comes mainly from the shoulder section of the steer, as well as parts of the neck, ribs, and the upper arm.
